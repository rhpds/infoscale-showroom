== Your role and mission

You're a Platform Engineer at the OpenMRS Foundation, a global open-source organization whose patient management systems power hospitals and clinics worldwide. Clinical staff in multiple countries depend on these systems 24/7 to access critical patient records, administer medication, and coordinate care.

The OpenMRS Foundation has invested in a dual-cluster OpenShift Virtualization environment -- a primary production cluster (`{primary_cluster}`) and a geographically separate disaster recovery cluster (`{secondary_cluster}`). Your manager has made the stakes clear: "We need near-zero data loss through continuous non-periodic replication and the ability to restore patient access within minutes of any infrastructure failure. Healthcare regulations require it, and patient safety depends on it."

**Your assignment**: Configure and validate a complete multi-site disaster recovery solution using InfoScale for Kubernetes, ensuring that critical healthcare VMs can failover seamlessly between clusters.

**The objective**: Demonstrate that the OpenMRS patient management platform can survive a complete cluster failure and remain available to clinical staff.

== Success criteria for your mission

By the end of this workshop, you'll have hands-on skills to address real-world healthcare DR requirements:

* Verify a healthy InfoScale for Kubernetes installation across both clusters -- confirm the DR foundation is solid
* Install and configure the InfoScale DR Manager -- enable cross-cluster DR orchestration
* Deploy the healthcare VM application on OpenShift Virtualization -- establish the workload to protect
* Configure asynchronous data replication using InfoScale CRDs -- protect patient data continuously
* Execute a manual DR failover -- prove the system works when it matters most
* Validate data consistency after failover -- confirm no patient records were lost

**Technical outcome**: A fully configured and tested DR pipeline protecting healthcare VM workloads across two OpenShift {ocp_version} clusters running InfoScale {infoscale_version}.

**Business benefit**: Compliance with healthcare uptime and data integrity regulations, and confidence that patient care systems remain available during infrastructure failures.

== Target audience

This workshop is designed for:

* Platform Engineers responsible for OpenShift cluster operations and DR strategy
* Storage Administrators managing InfoScale deployments in Kubernetes environments
* Site Reliability Engineers (SREs) building resilient healthcare platforms
* OpenShift and OpenShift Virtualization Architects evaluating enterprise DR solutions

You should have:

* Familiarity with the `oc` CLI and Kubernetes resource management
* Basic understanding of Kubernetes custom resources (CRDs)
* Understanding of storage concepts (PVCs, StorageClasses, replication)
* Cluster-admin access to both `{primary_cluster}` and `{secondary_cluster}`

== The OpenMRS Foundation's DR challenge

**The situation**: The OpenMRS Foundation operates patient management VMs critical to clinical workflows across partner hospitals and clinics worldwide.

**Regulatory context**: Healthcare data regulations require continuous availability and near-zero data loss through continuous non-periodic replication for patient records. A prolonged outage or data loss event carries serious compliance and patient safety consequences.

**Current risk areas**:

* **Single point of failure**: Without a tested DR plan, a cluster failure could take patient systems offline for hours
* **Data exposure**: Asynchronous replication must be configured correctly to prevent loss of recent patient records during failover
* **Recovery complexity**: Manual failover without Kubernetes-native tooling increases recovery time and human error risk
* **Compliance gaps**: Unvalidated DR procedures do not satisfy healthcare audit requirements

**The opportunity**: InfoScale for Kubernetes brings enterprise storage DR capabilities natively into OpenShift, allowing the Foundation to protect VM workloads using the same Kubernetes CRDs the team already uses for application management.
